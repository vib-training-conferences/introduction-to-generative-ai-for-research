# Exercise: Comparing Publisher Policies on Generative AI in Writing and Illustrations

This exercise will help you explore and critically evaluate how different scientific publishers address the use of Generative AI tools in manuscript writing and figure creation.
Grouping: in pairs
Time: 10 min

## **Step 1: Use Perplexity as a Chatbot**
Go to https://www.perplexity.ai/ and start a new chat session.  

## **Step 2: Formulate a Prompt**
Create a clear and specific one-shot prompt for Perplexity.

The RACE approach could be relevant here (Role, Action, Context, Expectation).

A list of publishers:
- Cambridge University Press
- Elsevier
- Emerald
- IEEE
- SAGE
- Science
- Springer Nature
- Taylor & Francis
- Wiley

You may adapt the prompt to include:
- **Scope**: Which publishers or journals to include.
- **Focus**: Writing, figures, or both.
- **Output format**: Table, bullet points, or narrative.

## **Step 3: Reflect with Your Partner**
Discuss the chatbot’s output with your partner. Reflect on:
- **Accuracy**: Are the policies correctly represented?
- **Completeness**: Are any major aspects missing?
- **Clarity**: Is the summary easy to understand and relevant?
- **Actionability**: Would this information help a researcher comply with policies?
- Think about other relevant aspects.

## **Step 4: Document Your Reflections**
Note **several but at least three reflections** in the [shared document](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1moMdhFwxmMovg0IYHkI30QiOKu_5vh6yFiOwiiv1F-s/edit?usp=sharing).  

**Optional Extension:** Validate the chatbot’s answer by checking official publisher websites.
